Road Racing

Road Racing in the Motorcycle world has a contriversial past and will continue to have a contriversial future. One of the most well known road races in the world is the Isle Of Man Tourist Trophy Race. Road racings popularity is the strongest in Ireland, being as it's one of the few places in the world to have legal competitive road racing. Like the name suggests it takes place on normal "public" roads, meaning racers are passing by curbs, street lamps and signs, spectators, walls, buildings, and more. The three largest road racing events are

  • Isle of Man TT
  • Ulster GP
  • Northwest 200

Moto GP

Moto GP is the considered the "top tier" of professional motorcycle racing in the world. Racing on tracks all around the world, while the biggest following of the sport is held in European countries such as Italy and Spain. One of the big differences compared to other Racing Competitions is that the manufacturers are competeing just as much as the riders. With a very strict rule setting each company is trying to build the best possible bike, by trying new things, bending the rules, and so on. There are 2 other categories in MotoGP

  • Moto 2
  • Moto 3

MotoGP or Moto 1 is raced on 1,000cc bikes, then Moto 2 is raced on 600cc bikes, while Moto 3 is raced on 250cc bikes. Generally you try to work your way up to MotoGP, but there is a wide array of ages in each class, the youngest being 15 in Moto 3 to the oldest being 37 in MotoGP

Motorcross

Motorcross Racing is one of, if not the, most popular professional off-road racing styles. Racers race from inside stadiums, to tracks in the desert, and everywhere in between.
